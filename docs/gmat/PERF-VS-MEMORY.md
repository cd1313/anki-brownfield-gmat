## What this shows

- **Memory barely moves** across the scenarios (range **0 pts**): it depends only on the (fixed) term reviews, not on MCQ answers.
- **Performance tracks MCQ accuracy** (range **57 pts**): it comes from the IRT model over the MCQ pool.
- Because the two scores are driven by different inputs, they **diverge** — the Memory−Performance gap grows as MCQ accuracy falls. A student who memorises term wording (high Memory) but can't answer new questions (low Performance) shows exactly this gap, instead of one number hiding the other.

**Verdict: PASS** — Memory is essentially flat while Performance spans the full range as question accuracy changes, so Performance is not echoing Memory.

## Honesty notes

- MCQ outcomes are simulated at a set accuracy to isolate the effect; the point is the **independence** of the two engine scores, which is a structural property of the models (different inputs), not a tuned result.
- Memory here is the practiced-recall score (term cards just reviewed → retrievability near 1.0); the small residual movement is FSRS/EAP noise, not a dependence on MCQs.
