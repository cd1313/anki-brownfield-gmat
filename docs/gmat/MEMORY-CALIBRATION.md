## Honesty notes

- The retrievability curve is the shipped engine's, validated above (max abs error ~1e-3). The **calibration data are simulated**, not real student reviews.
- Outcomes come from each card's _true_ stability; predictions from a _noisily estimated_ stability. The estimation-noise model (lognormal σ) is an assumption — disclosed — standing in for real finite-history fitting error.
- The perfect-estimation reference shows the metric floor when predictions equal the truth (reliability on the diagonal). The σ>0 column shows residual miscalibration under realistic estimation error.
- Real calibration on held-out student reviews is the honest next step (spec Step 4 bonus); we do not claim it here.
